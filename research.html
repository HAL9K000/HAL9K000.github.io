Socio-Ecological Tipping Points & Dryland Resilience

I explore the resilience of dryland ecosystems to desertification in the face of climate change, grazing pressures, land use and other anthropogenic pressures utilising a mix of theoretical, statistical and empirical data‐driven approaches. I primarily investigate the desertification process from the lens of complex systems theory and stochastic processes, with an emphasis on the role of dryland vegetation spatial patterns in predicting and understanding the underlying transitions. I do so using a mix of stochastic computational modelling and statistical analyses of satellite imagery.
